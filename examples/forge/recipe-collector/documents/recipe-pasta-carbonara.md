# Pasta Carbonara

Roman classic: spaghetti with guanciale, eggs, pecorino, and black pepper. No cream.

## INGREDIENTS

- 400 g spaghetti
- 150 g guanciale
- 4 large egg yolks
- 1 large whole egg
- 60 g pecorino romano
- 6 g black pepper
- 10 g salt

## STEPS

1. Cut guanciale into 5-mm strips. Render in a cold dry pan over medium heat until crisp. Reserve the fat.
2. Boil spaghetti in salted water until 1 minute short of al dente. Reserve 200 ml of pasta water.
3. Whisk yolks, whole egg, pecorino, and pepper into a thick paste.
4. Off heat, toss the drained pasta with the rendered guanciale. Add the egg paste and a splash of pasta water; toss vigorously until creamy.
5. Plate immediately, finish with extra pecorino and pepper.

## TIPS

- Pan must be off the heat when the eggs hit. Otherwise: scrambled eggs.
- No guanciale? Pancetta is the next best; bacon is a third-tier substitute.
- Pasta water is the emulsifier. If the sauce splits, add 20 ml at a time and toss.
