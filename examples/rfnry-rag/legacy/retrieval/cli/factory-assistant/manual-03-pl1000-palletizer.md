## 2. System Overview

The PL-1000 consists of five major assemblies:

**Infeed conveyor**: Receives cases from the upstream line. A photoeye (PE-01) detects case presence and triggers the diverter. The infeed conveyor speed is synchronized to the upstream line via a 4-20mA signal from the line PLC.

**Pattern forming table**: Cases are oriented and grouped into a single layer pattern. The pattern is defined in the HMI recipe system. Standard patterns include column stack, interlocking brick, and pinwheel. The pattern table uses servo-driven pushers (X-axis and Y-axis) to position cases. Pusher position accuracy: +/- 0.5 mm.

**Layer transfer**: A vacuum head with 24 suction cups picks up the completed layer from the pattern table and places it on the pallet or on top of the previous layer. The vacuum head travels on a gantry driven by servo motors (Z-axis and Y-axis travel). Vacuum is generated by a Venturi system requiring 80 PSI supply air.

**Pallet dispenser**: Stores up to 15 empty pallets. A clamp mechanism separates the bottom pallet from the stack and feeds it onto the build station conveyor. Pallet detection photoeye PE-05 confirms pallet presence before layer placement begins.

**Discharge conveyor**: Moves the completed pallet load to the stretch wrapper or pickup station. A full-load photoeye PE-08 signals the downstream equipment that a completed pallet is ready.

## 3. Pattern Configuration

### 3.1 Accessing the Recipe Editor

On the HMI, press MENU > RECIPES > EDIT. The recipe editor shows the current pattern as a top-down view of the pallet. Each case is represented as a rectangle that can be positioned, rotated 90 degrees, or mirrored.

### 3.2 Creating a New Pattern

1. Press NEW RECIPE and enter a name (max 16 characters)
2. Enter case dimensions: length, width, height (inches)
3. Enter pallet dimensions (select from preset or enter custom)
4. The HMI auto-generates an initial pattern. Modify as needed.
5. Set the number of layers per pallet
6. Set alternating pattern if using interlocking layers (odd/even layer flip)
7. Press SAVE. The new pattern is stored in the PLC recipe memory (capacity: 50 recipes).

### 3.3 Pattern Optimization

For maximum stability:
- Interlocking (brick) patterns resist lateral forces better than column stacks
- Alternate layer rotation by 90 or 180 degrees for cross-linking
- Keep the center of gravity centered on the pallet — avoid patterns that concentrate weight on one side
- For heavy cases (>40 lbs), use column stack to avoid crushing lower cases during transport

## 4. Safety Systems

### 4.1 Safety Circuit

The PL-1000 uses a Pilz PNOZ safety relay (part number SR-100) to monitor:
- Emergency stop buttons (3 locations: infeed, main panel, discharge)
- Light curtain on the operator access side (LC-01, Category 4, Type 4)
- Safety interlocks on all access doors (4 doors, magnetically coded switches)

Any safety device activation immediately:
1. Stops all servo motion
2. Releases the vacuum head (drops current load if mid-transfer — design ensures drop zone is within the guarded area)
3. Vents pneumatic pressure through the safety exhaust valve
4. Activates the red stack light and alarm horn

### 4.2 Resetting After Safety Stop

1. Identify and resolve the cause of the safety stop
2. Ensure all personnel are clear of the machine envelope
3. Close all access doors
4. Press and hold the RESET button on the main panel for 3 seconds
5. The HMI will display the recovery screen — follow the prompts to re-home the axes and resume operation

WARNING: The vacuum head weighs 180 lbs. Never enter the machine envelope while the head is raised. Even with power removed, a residual vacuum can release unexpectedly, dropping the head. Always engage the mechanical safety lock (yellow pin on the Z-axis cylinder) before any work under the raised head.

## 5. Maintenance

### 5.1 Daily Checklist

- Inspect suction cups for damage, contamination, or loss of elasticity. Replace any cup that doesn't spring back when pressed. Part number SC-24 (set of 24).
- Check air pressure at the regulator — must read 80 PSI minimum. Low pressure causes dropped layers.
- Verify all photoeyes are clean and aligned — wipe lenses with a soft cloth.
- Drain the air preparation unit (filter/regulator/lubricator) of accumulated water.
- Inspect the conveyor belts for debris, torn edges, or tracking issues.

### 5.2 Weekly Maintenance

- Grease all linear bearings on the gantry (8 points) with Mobilux EP2 lithium grease. Use the grease fittings — 3 pumps each.
- Check servo belt tension on X, Y, and Z axes. Belt should deflect 3mm with 2 kg force at the midpoint.
- Inspect the pallet dispenser clamps for wear. Clamp pads (part CP-100) should be replaced when worn to less than 3mm thickness.
- Run the HMI diagnostic page (MENU > DIAGNOSTICS > I/O STATUS) and verify all sensors show correct states.

### 5.3 Annual Maintenance

- Replace all suction cups (SC-24), regardless of appearance
- Replace all conveyor belts (IB-100, PB-100, DB-100)
- Inspect servo motors for unusual noise or vibration — contact service if detected
- Calibrate the layer transfer vacuum sensor (VS-01). The vacuum level during pickup should read 18-22 inHg. Below 18 inHg indicates a leak in the vacuum circuit.
- Test all safety devices per ISO 13849 requirements. Document results.

## 6. Troubleshooting

### Vacuum head drops cases during transfer
1. Check vacuum level on the HMI diagnostic page — should read 18-22 inHg during pickup
2. Inspect suction cups for damage — even one failed cup can cause uneven vacuum distribution
3. Check the Venturi generator for blockage — disassemble and clean with compressed air
4. Verify air supply pressure is 80 PSI at the machine regulator
5. Check vacuum hoses for kinks, cracks, or loose fittings

### Cases are misaligned on the pallet
1. Verify recipe dimensions match actual case dimensions — measure with a tape
2. Check the pattern table pusher calibration — run MENU > DIAGNOSTICS > SERVO HOME to re-home axes
3. Inspect pusher pads for wear — worn pads allow cases to shift during contact
4. Check the layer transfer head for level — use a spirit level on the vacuum plate. Adjust with the four leveling screws on the gantry mount.

### Pallet dispenser jams
1. Check for damaged pallets — broken stringers or protruding nails catch on the clamps
2. Verify pallet stack is aligned — pallets must be stacked squarely in the dispenser magazine
3. Inspect clamp pads CP-100 for wear — worn pads cannot grip the pallet reliably
4. Check the separation cylinder stroke — if the cylinder doesn't fully extend, it may not clear the second pallet
