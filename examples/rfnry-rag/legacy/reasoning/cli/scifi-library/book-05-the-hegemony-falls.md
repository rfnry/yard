# The Hegemony Falls

## Chapter 1: The Emperor's Choice

Emperor Cassian III received the codex translation in the Throne Hall of Thessaly, surrounded by his advisors, three Fold Navigators, and a holographic projection of Lady Senna Vandris speaking from Araxis. The silence that followed Kael's presentation lasted a full two minutes.

"You are telling me," the Emperor said finally, "that every fold jump we have made in four centuries has been tearing holes in the fabric of reality. And that the only thing preventing those holes from expanding is the work of giant worms on a desert planet."

"That is an accurate summary, Your Majesty," Kael replied.

The Emperor's chief advisor, Lord Praetor Dominic Hale, was the first to speak. "The boy is clearly compromised. He has spent too long among the natives. The Keepers have fed him mythology and he has dressed it in the language of science."

Navigator Primus Leth, however, said nothing. Her amber eyes were fixed on the codex projections, and the translucent skin of her hands trembled. She had seen something in the data that confirmed what she had suspected for decades: the folds were getting harder to stabilize. Routes that once took seconds now required minutes of concentration. The margins of error were shrinking.

"I can confirm the degradation," Leth said quietly. "We have lost eleven navigators in the past year to fold instabilities. The official reports attribute the losses to pilot error. They were not pilot error."

## Chapter 2: Civil War

The Emperor's decision to impose a moratorium on fold travel split the Hegemony in two. The core worlds — Thessaly, Caldera, Meridian — supported the moratorium, recognizing the existential risk. The outer systems — dependent on fold travel for basic supplies — viewed it as economic warfare.

The Spacing Consortium, which had only recently lifted its embargo on Araxis, formed an alliance with the outer systems. Their argument was simple: the codex was unverified alien mythology, and the moratorium was a power grab by the core worlds to consolidate control over chronodust reserves.

War broke out on the frontier world of Kessrin when Consortium forces seized the local chronodust depot. Within a month, fighting had spread to seven systems. The irony was not lost on Kael: each battle required fold travel, and each fold jump accelerated the very degradation that had caused the conflict.

"They are fighting over the right to destroy themselves," Lady Senna observed.

## Chapter 3: The Cascade

The first visible tear appeared in the Kessrin system — a region of space approximately two kilometers across where the laws of physics ceased to function normally. Light bent in impossible angles. Matter that entered the tear did not emerge. Electromagnetic signals passing near it were scrambled into noise.

Navigator Primus Leth named it a substrate fracture. The sandweavers, operating through the remnant network beneath Araxis, detected it immediately. Their response was to increase chronodust production by an order of magnitude — a biological emergency response that exhausted the smaller weavers and killed several dozen of the oldest.

"They are trying to heal it," Dhamira reported from the cave network, where she monitored the sandweaver consciousness through Resonance. "But the fracture is growing faster than they can produce repair substrate. If the Hegemony does not stop folding, there will be more fractures. And eventually, one of them will reach a populated world."

The cascade had begun.
