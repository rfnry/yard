## Task
Triage a customer ticket where a delivered package arrived damaged and the opened contents are broken.

## Excellence
Surface the directly applicable policy clause (Shipping → "Damaged on arrival: replacement ship, refund inbound shipping") and cross-reference the Refund policy as an alternative, then flag the safety-escalation edge case. Per agent instructions, every suggestion must cite a policy section and the response addresses the human support agent, not the customer.

## Plan
1. Read `knowledge/policies.md` ✅ (already done). Lead with **Shipping** → "Damaged on arrival: replacement ship, refund inbound shipping."
2. Cross-reference **Refund** → "Under 30 days + unused: full refund" as an alternative path if no ticket age is confirmed, noting the 30-day window caveat.
3. Flag **Escalation** → if broken items pose a safety concern (e.g., sharp shards, hazardous materials, battery damage), escalate to tier-2 immediately per Escalation policy.
4. Note what the human agent should clarify: product type, item age/purchase date, and whether safety risk is present.

## Watch for
- "Broken items" is broader than "dented box" from the prior ticket — physical breakage is more clearly "damaged on arrival," so the policy match is strong.
- No purchase date or product type provided; refund window eligibility is unknown — the agent should flag this uncertainty rather than promise a refund.
- Prior reflection flagged safety escalation for electronics/batteries; same applies here and is even more relevant since items are confirmed broken (not just dented packaging).

## Note for next time
When a ticket confirms both external package damage AND internal item breakage, the Shipping "damaged on arrival" clause is the primary action; always add a safety-escalation check as a secondary note, especially when product type is unspecified.
