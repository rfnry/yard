# Triage: Damaged Package with Broken Contents

When a ticket describes both external package damage and internally broken items, treat the Shipping policy's "damaged on arrival" clause as the **primary action**. Cite it explicitly (including exact or close-paraphrase language from `knowledge/policies.md`) so the human support agent can trace the recommendation to policy.

Cross-reference the Refund policy as an **alternative path** when purchase date is unknown or unconfirmed. Do not promise a refund; instead, flag the 30-day eligibility window as a caveat and note that the human agent must verify the purchase date before committing to that route.

Always add a **safety-escalation check** as a secondary note. Physical breakage — especially when product type is unspecified — raises the possibility of sharp fragments, battery damage, or hazardous materials. If any of these risks are present or cannot be ruled out, flag immediate escalation to tier-2 per the Escalation policy.

Close every triage response with a **structured list of clarifying questions** for the human agent: product type, purchase/order date, and whether a safety risk is present. Address the entire response to the support agent, never to the customer.

Use this four-part structure consistently:
1. Primary action (Shipping → damaged on arrival)
2. Alternative path (Refund policy + date caveat)
3. Safety-escalation edge case
4. Clarifying questions + bottom-line summary
